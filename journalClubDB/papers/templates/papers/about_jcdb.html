What is journalClubDB?

The "journal club" is an integral component of the PhD experience. In journal clubs, students share and discuss recently published academic papers. Journal clubs are tradiationally conducted via face-to-face interaction. JournalClubDB provides functionality to discuss scientific publications online.

Note that JournalClubDB is currently in beta. There is a good chance that I will need to wipe the database in order to implement new functionality, so please be aware that any online discussions on JCDB are subject to deletion without warning.


How do I get started?

The first thing you want to do is log in. Simply click Login at the top right-hand corner of the screen. Then, choose whether you want to use your Google, Facebook, or Twitter account to authenticate.

Next, import the publication you want to discuss into JCDB. On the top of the screen, click "Search -> Import papers from pubmed"

The rest is self-explanatory (hopefully).


Why do I have to use google/facebook/twitter to log into JCDB? Is there an option to create a JCDB account without using social media?

We hope that encouraging users to discuss papers using their real name (or at least their official social media account) will foster high-quality discussions. At the same time, we recognize that the value of user-controlled anonymity. If you feel uncomfortable engaging in discussion with your real name, you can create a throw-away JCDB account unlinked to social media here .

P.S. You will need to come back here to use the conventional login system each time. We are really trying to encourage users to use the social media authentication system.


What are "Paper Highlights". Can I submit my own publications as a "Paper Highlight"?

Paper Highlights are featured on the front page of JCDB. We would be happy to host your publication as a paper highlight if you are willing to write a short layman's summary of your work. Please contact us to work out the details.


I'm a physicist/chemist/mathematician, but can't find any physics/chemistry/mathematics articles on JournalClubDB. Why is this?

JournalClubDB uses pubmed's search engine to import publications. Pubmed only indexes publications supported by NIH funding, which means only biological articles can be imported into JCDB. We are looking into alternative search engines, but for now JCDB can only be used for biological research articles.
